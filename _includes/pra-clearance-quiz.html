Find out if you will need PRA clearance for RESEARCH

Answer the following questions about your RESEARCH PARTICIPANTS:

Will you be conducting research with 10 or more participants (per "round" of research)?
For example, if you are interviewing participants, conducting usability testing and then sending out a post-launch feedback survey in the course of developing a new product, each of these activities would be considered different "rounds" of research because they are intended to collect different information.
Will ANY of your research participants be members of the public?
A member of the public is anyone who is not a federal employee or contractor acting within the scope of their employment.

Answer the following questions about your RESEARCH METHODS:

Will your research involve direct observation?
That is, will you or someone on your team gather information by observing participants in person, virtually or on a recording?
Will you be posing a set of close-ended questions to participants?
Close-ended questions have a limited range of answers (for example, "How would you rate your experience on a scale of 1-5?"), while open-ended questions do not limit participant answers (for example, "Please provide feedback on your experience using this website.")
